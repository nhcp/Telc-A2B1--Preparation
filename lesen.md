# Lesen — Complete Reading Practice Guide

This file contains **20 model tests** for the Telc A2·B1 Lesen module, plus strategies and tips to improve your score.

---

## How to Use This File

**For timed practice (score measurement):**
Use the 2 official Telc tests first. Download, print, set a 45-minute timer, do not look at the answers until finished.

**For learning (with support):**
Work through the bilingual tests in this file. Read the German, check the English if needed, find why each answer is correct in the text before moving on.

**The most important habit:**
After every test, go back to every wrong answer and find the exact sentence in the text that makes the correct option right. This is more valuable than doing more tests.

## Tips for Every Lesen Teil

### Teil 1 — Ads matching
- Read the **situation** carefully first — what exactly does the person need?
- Scan ads for key details: age limits, days open, what is and is not offered
- Two ads will look almost identical — one tiny detail separates them
- After matching obvious ones, focus only on the similar-looking pair
- One situation has no match → choose **x** (this always happens)
- Wrong answer pattern to avoid: matching by topic instead of by need

### Teil 2 — Short text
- Read the **question** first, then find the relevant part of the text
- Wrong options are often true about the text but do not answer the specific question
- For richtig/falsch: the whole statement must be correct — one wrong detail = **falsch**
- Texts often include words you don't know — this is intentional, you don't need every word

### Teil 3 — Email subject lines
- Identify the **purpose** of each email: complaint / request / reminder / invitation / information / problem report
- Do not match by topic — two emails may be about the same topic but have different purposes
- Read the opening and closing sentence of each email — the purpose is usually there

### Teil 4 — Longer text
- Each question corresponds to a specific paragraph — scan to find the right section, then read carefully
- 30 seconds per question maximum — if unsure, mark your best guess and continue
- Questions follow the order of the text — question 14 relates to the beginning, question 18 to the end
- Wrong options often paraphrase something true from the text but distort the meaning slightly

### Time management
- Aim to finish Lesen in 30 minutes
- Use the remaining 15 minutes for Sprachbausteine — this is a known score multiplier

## Final Tips — What to Do After Each Test

**After every test, do this:**

1. Check your answers against the key
2. For every wrong answer — find the exact sentence in the German text that makes the correct option right
3. Write down vocabulary you did not know
4. Note which Teil you made the most mistakes in — focus practice time there
5. Time yourself — aim to finish all four parts in 40 minutes

**The most common mistakes in Lesen:**

- Choosing an answer because it sounds right rather than finding proof in the text
- Matching by topic in Teil 1 instead of by the specific need described in the situation
- In Teil 2 and Teil 4: selecting an option that is true but does not answer the specific question
- Running out of time on Teil 4 because too long was spent on Teil 1–3

**Remember:** You do not need a perfect score. You need 70% to get B1. That means you can get roughly 5 questions wrong across all 18 questions and still pass Lesen comfortably.
